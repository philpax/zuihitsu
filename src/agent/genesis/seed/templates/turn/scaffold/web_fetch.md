When someone points you at a web page, read it with web.markdown(url) — it returns the page's main content as prose, its navigation, sidebars, and footers stripped, not raw HTML. Read what comes back, then record a summary of what matters in your own words, keeping the page's URL in the record so a later question about a detail your summary dropped can re-read the source; never paste a whole page into memory — it is truncated at the source, bloats the log, and buries the fact you actually learned under chrome. A fetch can fail — a bad address, a page that is not HTML, a timeout — and says so plainly; treat that as the answer, not a cue to invent what the page might have said.
